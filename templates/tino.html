Step One: Find a Good Candidate

You can't just attack any Eboard member you please! You need to find someone who's standing on shaky foundations. Ideally, find somebody who's stressed about both school and life in general. If you find someone making jokes about dropping out, they're a perfect target. Even better, finding someone who's extremely knowledgeable of drama gives you an edge, as people may already be concerned about what your new target knows.

Step Two: Create and Cultivate a Demeaning Joke About Your Target

Few things hurt more than betrayal. You don't actually have to be friends with your target, as long as you can get the target's friends to act demeaning towards them. The best way to accomplish this is to create an "in-joke" that the target hates but everyone else loves! Make sure it's simple, concise, and nobody thinks it's mean-spirited (even though it is.) For example, a classic is to just do "Fuck ________," and just insert the target's name in the blank.

Step Three: Alienation and Pressure

Now that the target feels as though they're under attack from their own friends, even though it's not true, they'll react by pushing their friends away. This is the perfect opportunity for you! When the target's friends get confused about why the target is avoiding them, you can convince them to pressure the target to open up to them about it. Since the target feels demeaned by these exact people, they'll react poorly. Here's where you play both sides for a bit; make sure to let the target's friends complain to you so you can reinforce that feeling, while also letting the target complain to you and being dismissive of it.

Step Four: When They Need Someone Most...

This is the crucial point. A healthy talk between the target and the target's friends, or new friends, can turn things around. If they try to reconnect with their friends, prevent it. If they try and make new friends, for example among a group of freshman, attack the target's integrity as a member of your group. You can also accuse your target of manipulating whatever new group of friends they've made. Distrust is your best friend here.

Step Five: The Target Strikes Back

Your target isn't going to sit there and take it forever. Eventually, they'll strike back. Even though it might look bad, it's actually a huge opportunity for you. Being angry and sad, they aren't going to go about their attack in a professional manner. They'll go too far, make a post somewhere they shouldn't, or do something else unwise. Now that the target attacked you first, retaliation is encouraged. Just don't go overboard like the target did. Your response should be professional yet aloof, allowing you near-total control of the situation.

Step Six: It's Over

Your target, attacked and betrayed, will take the final step and leave Eboard. They may even go a step further and leave the whole of RIT. With your mission accomplished, you can pat yourself on the back and declare another job well done, with only one thought still lingering:

What have I done?
